Garrison

gar·ri·son

/ˈɡarɪsən/  ·  noun  ·  [O.Fr. garison, “defence, provision”; cf. L. praesidium]

A fortified post where a body of troops is stationed to hold and defend it.

In Garrison: the local base where your operatives (AI agents you configure) are stationed: faculties (capability slots) filled by swappable fittings, an Orchestrator directing, all quartered over your own Claude Code / Codex / Agent SDK setup (or any other CLI/SDK) in the Quarters. Composed the way veterans run it.

01 · The base

The structured base for your whole AI ecosystem.

Working with AI agents means keeping up with too much: which tools are best right now, which MCPs, which models, which skills, what each can actually do. You need expertise in five areas to get good work out of one agent.

Then comes the juggling: platforms, tools, providers, and models, each with its own setup, accounts, and way of doing things.

No single platform solves this, and none is coming soon. The big providers (Anthropic, OpenAI) build generalist tools that don't bend to your workflow; the small ones do one thing well and nothing else.

Garrison is the base where it all comes together. Pick the tools, providers, and harnesses you want; Garrison makes them work as one system. Start from curated compositions that veterans run daily, then change anything: adjust by configuration, or clone a part and redo it. Opinionated, never rigid.

A composition is a chosen set of fittings filling the faculties: reasonable defaults, fully swappable, customizable with no development knowledge.

02 · The ranks

Who Garrison is for.

developers

Switch models, runtimes, effort, and skills per task; build whole new profiles and flows. Experiment fast, keep the workflow you have.

builders

Assemble reliable agents from vetted parts: pick a fitting from a plain description and let the assistant wire it up.

end-users

Run a personal operative on your own machine: memory, scheduling, tasks, and actions. An assistant that is yours, not a black box.

03 · The charter

Standing Orders

Ten articles the garrison follows without question.

I

Operatives are model-agnostic.

The Orchestrator, the operative's single entry point, routes each task to the right runtime, model, effort, and skills. Any runtime can hold the primary post; the rest take delegations.

II

Operatives are secure.

Every fitting is a scanned, vetted APM package; secrets are Vault-sealed with AES-256-GCM and never logged.APM is Microsoft's package manager for agent parts: npm, but for skills, access, and memory.

III

Opinionated, but completely flexible.

Garrison ships the defaults of the daily AI ecosystem; swapping a fitting is one click.

IV

Everything is transparent and observable.

Every action is logged and visible; every faculty, fitting, and setting carries a plain description.

V

Every fitting fulfils a faculty contract.

One contract lets parts in any language or runtime fill the same slot; the operative never asks how a part was built.

VI

Compose, don't own.

Garrison arranges your real ~/.claude, ~/.codex, ~/.gemini; it never replaces your tools or traps you in a runtime of its own.

VII

Local-first. Your machine, your data, your keys.

Operatives run on your hardware; secrets, memory, and logs stay with you.

VIII

The system improves itself.

The Improver reads every session, sharpening your skills, routing, and memory with use.

IX

One base, many outposts.

Work fans out to outposts: your other machines, cloud boxes, containers. Every outpost shares the base's memory, coordination, codebase, vault keys, and improvements.

X

Open by default.

MIT-licensed, auditable end to end, no lock-in.

04 · The pains

Four pains, one seed composition.

Each pain below is solved by the current seed composition; every part of it can be swapped. Panels are illustrative; the bullets are what runs today.

Pain 1 · Coordination

Parallel sessions that know nothing about each other.

You open a second session and it immediately steps on the first. You end up serializing work, or babysitting branches.

The seed composition:

  • Every task is a visible card on the Kanban Loop: dev tasks, personal tasks, manual work, one board.
  • A coordination fitting lets agents see what the others are doing and build on each other's work.
  • Everything runs on the main branch. No worktrees needed: cards and coordination make trampling impossible.
  • A heartbeat picks up finished cards and tests them together in one batch.
  • Plans gate builds; evidence lands on the card at every stage: logs, screenshots, videos you can judge from your phone.

Prefer worktrees? That exists as an alternative composition. Use what veterans run, adjust it, or build your own, vetted through APM.

Pain 2 · Capacity

Juggling accounts, providers, limits, and machines.

Usage caps hit mid-task. You hold accounts on several providers and switch by hand. Your other machines sit idle.

The seed composition:

  • Runtime fittings for Claude Code on a warm PTY pool, Agent SDK, Codex, Gemini CLI, opencode, and any runtime you add; any of them can be the primary.
  • The Orchestrator classifies each task and picks runtime, model, and effort by rule, automatically or by your override.
  • Register several accounts; the Paymaster hands each session the least-used one, holds when all are capped, and flags dead tokens for re-login.
  • Swap the whole policy by switching composition: cheap, quality, or balanced daily.
  • Outposts link your other machines: run commands, read and write files, mirror directories on a schedule.
  • Memory travels with you: the Obsidian vault git-syncs across machines, shared by every runtime and agent.

All defaults. Use the routing and account policy veterans run, adjust it, or build your own, vetted through APM.

Pain 3 · Testing

Being the test browser yourself.

Every change means opening the browser, logging in again, clicking through the same flow to see what broke.

The seed composition:

  • Drill plans tests as visible, editable steps: a Drill Book per page of your app, diffable in the repo.
  • Give feedback on any step and see exactly what happened in it, run by run.
  • Provide authentication once; runs log in by themselves and stop stumbling on logins.
  • Browser automation with vision runs the steps; assertions graduate into a repeatable e2e suite that catches regressions without you watching.

The seed default. Use the QA flow veterans run, adjust it, or build your own, vetted through APM.

Pain 4 · Presence

Work stopping when you leave the desk.

You step away and everything waits for you.

The seed composition:

  • The Web Channel: responsive, mobile-first chat over your operative. Three hats: Gary the assistant, Joe the developer, James the product manager.
  • Discuss a task with James until the brief is right, queue it, or run something simple immediately.
  • Type, talk, or listen: local or Deepgram voice with silence detection; replies and documents read aloud.
  • Schedule and automate. Every card stays on the Kanban Loop, evidence comes back to the channel, and you review results without retesting.

Also swappable: a Slack channel instead, or whatever veterans run next; adjust it or build your own, vetted through APM.

05 · The veterans

Use what the veterans use.

Every composition and fitting is a package other builders can battle-test and pass on. Adopt what veterans run daily, adjust one that almost fits, or build your own; every package goes through APM security scanning. The seed composition is the first veteran composition. Yours can be next.
