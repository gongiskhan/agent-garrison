Garrison

gar·ri·son

/ˈɡarɪsən/  ·  noun  ·  [O.Fr. garison, “defence, provision”; cf. L. praesidium]

A fortified post where a body of troops is stationed to hold and defend it.

In Garrison: the local base where your operatives (AI agents you configure) are stationed: faculties (capability slots) filled by swappable fittings, an Orchestrator directing, all quartered over your own Claude Code setup in the Quarters.

01 · The base

The structured base for your whole AI development setup.

Every tool, model, runtime, skill, and setting in one place, swappable in a click. Try anything new without rebuilding your workflow; assemble agent systems like Hermes or OpenClaw, reined in and transparent. Platform, CLI, and model agnostic.

02 · The ranks

Who Garrison is for.

developers

Switch models, runtimes, effort, and skills per task; build whole new profiles and flows. Experiment fast, keep the workflow you have.

builders

Assemble reliable agents from vetted parts: pick a fitting from a plain description and let the assistant wire it up.

end-users

Run a personal operative on your own machine: memory, scheduling, tasks, and actions. An assistant that is yours, not a black box.

03 · The charter

Standing Orders

Ten articles the garrison follows without question.

I

Operatives are model-agnostic.

The Orchestrator, the operative's single entry point, routes each task to the right runtime, model, effort, and skills.

II

Operatives are secure.

Every fitting is a scanned, vetted APM package; secrets are Vault-sealed with AES-256-GCM and never logged.APM is Microsoft's package manager for agent parts: npm, but for skills, access, and memory.

III

Opinionated, but completely flexible.

Garrison ships the defaults of daily AI development; swapping a fitting is one click.

IV

Everything is transparent and observable.

Every action is logged and visible; every faculty, fitting, and setting carries a plain description.

V

Every fitting fulfils a faculty contract.

One contract lets parts in any language or runtime fill the same slot; the operative never asks how a part was built.

VI

Compose, don't own.

Garrison arranges your real ~/.claude; it never replaces your tools or traps you in a runtime of its own.

VII

Local-first. Your machine, your data, your keys.

Operatives run on your hardware; secrets, memory, and logs stay with you.

VIII

The system improves itself.

The Improver reads every session, sharpening your skills, routing, and memory with use.

IX

Plan before you build.

On the Kanban Loop a plan gates every build, so nothing runs off on its own.

X

Open by default.

MIT-licensed, auditable end to end, no lock-in.

04 · The fittings

Three flagship fittings.

The panels are illustrative. The bullets are what runs today.

Web Channel

One chat, three hats.

  • Mobile-first chat over your operative. Start a message with a name to switch hats: Gary, Joe, or James.
  • Gary is the personal assistant. Knows your memories, your work, your personal life. Manages and schedules tasks and puts it all together.
  • Joe is the software developer and automations expert. Works your projects folder: plans, executes, runs autonomous flows, and leaves evidence with links to videos you can watch from the phone after being away.
  • James is the product manager and architect. Knows your projects and products, discusses new features with you until you are satisfied, and produces the documents Joe then builds from.
  • Voice in, voice out, at no cost: local STT and TTS with silence detection. Talk to it interactively; have replies and documents read aloud.
  • Shows what is relevant, not the raw session. Drill into detail only when you want it.
  • Enable or disable parts of the flow through Orchestrator rules.

Kanban Loop

Autonomous development you can watch from your phone.

  • One board for everything: dev tasks, personal tasks, manual work. The visual face of the AutoThink skill that ships in the seed composition.
  • Discuss with James on the card and produce the brief. Or skip straight from To Do to Plan and let it run fully autonomous.
  • Plan uses your instincts, collected by the Improver from your sessions and memories. Over time plans come out the way you would have written them, without intervention.
  • Implement with evidence for everything: logs linked from the card, viewable in the interface, editable in a built-in Monaco editor, screenshots where relevant.
  • Review includes a security audit. Then adversarial review. Then automated tests, e2e tests written from them, then adversarial tests. Evidence collected at every stage, linked on the card.
  • Walkthrough produces videos of what was done, in the browser and over highlighted logs, verifiable end to end. Good enough to hand to someone as a demo.
  • Mobile-first. Follow development from your phone with every link and every piece of evidence. Tailscale is detected and recommended for easy access from anywhere.
  • Done is where you judge the work. Not good? Drag the card back to any stage. Blocked work self-heals when possible, otherwise it lands in Needs Attention.

Dev Env

Your sessions, your browser, your terminals. One pane of glass.

  • See every Claude Code session (or other runtime) that is running. Start sessions on main or in worktrees.
  • Parallel sessions do not trample each other: the seed composition's coordination tools handle it, on main or across worktrees.
  • Per session, choose plain Claude Code or the Garrison Orchestrator. The model router picks effort, model, and runtime per task by rule.
  • Runtimes today: Claude Code on a warm pool of PTY sessions, Agent SDK, Codex, Gemini CLI, and any runtime you add.
  • A browser pane wired to the session over CDP. The session sees what you see and can drive it. No screenshots, no leaving the environment.
  • Open more terminals and arrange them as panes. tmux-backed persistent sessions survive a reboot and can be revived, with full session history.
