Garrison

gar·ri·son

/ˈɡarɪsən/  ·  noun  ·  [O.Fr. garison, "defence, provision"; cf. L. praesidium]

A fortified post where a body of troops is stationed to hold and defend it.

In Garrison: the local base where your operatives (AI agents you configure) are stationed. Each is built from faculties (capability slots) filled by fittings (swappable parts you install from the Fittings Registry), directed by an orchestrator, and quartered over your own Claude Code setup in the Quarters.

01 · The base

Garrison is the structured base for your whole AI development setup. Every tool, model, runtime, skill, and setting in one place, swappable in a click, so you can try anything new in the AI ecosystem without rebuilding your workflow. Platform, CLI, and model agnostic.

Assemble agent systems like Hermes or OpenClaw, but reined in. Garrison operatives are safer, fully transparent, more configurable and easier to configure, more flexible, and aligned to what you actually want, instead of trying to do everything on their own.

02 · The ranks

Who Garrison is for.

developers

Structure around your day-to-day AI work. Switch models, runtimes, effort, and skills per task. Build whole new profiles and flows. Every tool and setting in one base, platform and CLI agnostic. Experiment fast, keep the workflow you already have.

builders

Assemble proven, reliable agents from parts developers already vetted, with no deep technical work. Read a plain description, pick a fitting, let the assistant wire it up. Ship full agents like Hermes or OpenClaw, shaped to the approaches you trust.

end-users

Run your own personal operative on your own machine. Memory, scheduling, tasks, and actions, powered by the latest AI tools that builders and developers packaged for you. An assistant that is yours, not a black box.

03 · The charter

Standing Orders

Ten articles the garrison follows without question.

I

Operatives are model-agnostic.

Pick the runtime, model, effort, and skills per task. The orchestrator, the single entry point for everything an operative does, routes each job to the right one.

II

Operatives are secure.

Every fitting is an APM package, scanned and vetted before it enters Garrison. Secrets are Vault-sealed with AES-256-GCM, scoped to one connector, and never written to a log.APM is Microsoft's package manager for agent parts. Think npm for Node, but for an agent's skills, access, and memory.

III

Opinionated, but completely flexible.

Garrison ships the defaults developers reach for when they build with AI every day. Disagree with one? Swapping a fitting is unselecting one and selecting another in the UI.

IV

Everything is transparent and observable.

Every action an operative takes is logged and visible. Every faculty, fitting, and setting shows in the UI with a plain description of what it does and how to configure it.

V

Every fitting fulfils a faculty contract.

That single contract is how parts written in different languages and runtimes talk to each other. Fill a slot with anything that honours the contract, and the rest of the operative does not care how it was built.

VI

Compose, don't own.

Garrison sits over your real ~/.claude and arranges it. It does not replace your tools, hide them, or trap you in a runtime of its own.

VII

Local-first. Your machine, your data, your keys.

Operatives run on your hardware, secrets live in your keychain, and memory and logs stay with you.

VIII

The system improves itself.

The Improver reads every session and sharpens your skills, your routing, and your memory. The more you use Garrison, the better it fits you.

IX

Plan before you build.

Work moves through discuss, then plan, then implement, on the Kanban Loop. The plan gates the build, so nothing runs off on its own.

X

Open by default.

MIT-licensed, auditable end to end, no lock-in.
